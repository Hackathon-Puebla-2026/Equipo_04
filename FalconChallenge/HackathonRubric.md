# Technical Evaluation Rubric

This rubric defines the evaluation criteria, their respective weights, and what is expected for each category.

---

## 1. Problem Formulation (25%)

### What will be evaluated
- Whether the problem is clearly and specifically defined.
- Whether the problem statement is supported by relevant literature.
- Whether there is a clear connection to the selected Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) target.
- Whether the societal challenge being addressed is well explained.
- Whether the expected societal impact of the proposed solution is clearly justified.

---

## 2. Baseline (20%)

### What will be evaluated
- The use of valid evaluation metrics.
- The use of recent findings or state-of-the-art references as the baseline for comparison.

---

## 3. Quantum Implementation (25%)

### What will be evaluated
- Correct implementation of the quantum solution.
- Clean and well-organized code.
- Proper code documentation.
- A justified explanation of why the chosen quantum approach is appropriate for the problem.

---

## 4. Benchmarking (20%)

### What will be evaluated
- Analysis of how the solution scales.
- Direct comparison between the proposed quantum approach and the selected baseline.
- Evidence that the comparison is meaningful and supported by results.

---

## 5. Quality of Presentation (10%)

### What will be evaluated
- Demonstrated understanding of the challenge.
- Clear explanation of the obtained results.
- Discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed solution.
- Ability to communicate the technical work effectively.

---

# Additional Requirements

- Explain any use of AI during the development of the project.
- Presentation slides **must be in English**.
- The oral presentation **may be given in either English or Spanish**.

---

## Evaluation Summary

- **Problem Formulation:** 25%
- **Baseline:** 20%
- **Quantum Implementation:** 25%
- **Benchmarking:** 20%
- **Quality of Presentation:** 10%
