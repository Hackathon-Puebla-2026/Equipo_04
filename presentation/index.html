Opening: Falcon is a binational reservoir on the US–Mexico border, in drought all year. Can data-driven optimization redistribute weekly releases to improve resilience, and can quantum methods encode it? Three headline numbers: the official 26-week benchmark, a feasible +0.021 SRS gain, exact optimum in 20 ms.
Why it matters

A shared river under multi-year drought

The Falcon Reservoir sat below its 25% conservation threshold every day of a 6-year record, and its release timing affects communities on both sides of the border.

Scarcity. Storage ~10–20% of capacity; 0 / 2182 days above Smin.
Cooperation. A US–Mexico treaty governs releases (SDG 6.5).
Resilience. Better timing buffers drought & climate stress (SDG 6.4 · 13.1).
Why it matters
Human framing - tell the story, don't read the tags: a binational, chronically depleted reservoir whose schedule affects real farms, cities and ecosystems. Optimization here squeezes resilience out of scarcity while honoring a shared-water agreement. The chart proves the drought is real and persistent, which is exactly why the critical-storage penalty C_crit dominates the objective.
Problem formulation

Optimize the weekly release adjustment u(t)

R(t) = R_obs(t) + u(t)   (u=0 = historical)  ·  SRS = −( w₁·C_crit + w₂·C_dev + w₃·C_smooth )

C_crit

penalizes weeks with S(t) < Smin (25% of Smax) → prevents depletion

C_dev

penalizes deviation from historical releases → operational realism

C_smooth

penalizes abrupt week-to-week change → deliverable policy

Constraints: R(t) ≥ 0 · |u(t)| ≤ umax · 0 ≤ S(t) ≤ Smax · cumulative balance |Σu| ≤ η·ΣRobs.

Formulation
Key: we optimize the adjustment, so u=0 is exactly historical operation, and every result is a clean delta vs. reality. Maximize SRS ≡ minimize penalized cost. The four constraints are enforced; the binding one turns out to be the cumulative balance budget.

Data integrity
Integrity story: the official Change-in-Storage series uses the opposite sign to the balance equation; used raw it drives storage negative. Our derived ΔS = S(t+1)−S(t) equals −(official) at weekly resolution exactly (corr +1.000, err ≈ 0), so we drop the "preliminary" caveat. Smax confirmed two independent ways. Release = 15-min discharge integrated to weekly volume.
Modeling · quantum implementation

Every relaxation is measured, not assumed

We keep only the constraints that actually bind in the data, so the QUBO is small and provably lossless.

Storage bounds: never bind → dropped (0 qubits).
Balance |Σu|≤B: binds ~97–99% → exact 1-slack.
R≥0 / one-hot: forbidden-level penalty / XY-mixer.
Constraint relaxations
Rubric centerpiece (Quantum 25%): the honest reason our QUBO is small is that we measured which constraints bind. Balance utilization |Σu|/B ≈ 100% on medium/large (it binds); debug/small are u=0 in the drought so balance is slack there. Storage never comes within a safety margin of 0 or S_max. This is "we dropped constraints that provably never act," not "we dropped constraints to save qubits."
Modeling · why the storage bounds drop

Storage never nears either limit

Across the whole record the reservoir sits far below Smax and never touches 0, so the bounds 0 ≤ S ≤ Smax never act. Dropping them costs nothing and shrinks the quantum problem.

Far from full. Peak storage is a small fraction of Smax.
Never empty. Storage stays well above 0 the entire year.
So: the two bounds are inactive → dropped, 0 extra qubits.
Storage bounds relaxation
Visual proof for the "storage bounds" caption on the previous slide: the observed trajectory lives deep inside the feasible band [0, Smax], never within a safety margin of either edge. That is why 0 ≤ S ≤ Smax is provably inactive and can be removed with zero loss, before any qubit is spent. Complements A1/A3 (which shows the balance budget is the constraint that actually binds).
Baseline

Compare only among feasible methods

A taller bar can be infeasible: "better" SRS only by overspending the balance budget. Read the green bars; exact DP is the honest strong baseline.

Baseline
Feasibility ≠ quality, independent axes. Feasible = obeys all 4 constraints (binding one = balance); SRS = objective value. A feasible schedule can be worse than historical (legit, drought); an infeasible one can look "better" only by cheating the budget (e.g. medium threshold-pure −0.289 but |Σu|−B > 0). That's why every bar is feasibility-coded. Balanced threshold ties DP on medium; DP is what the quantum method is judged against.
Classical baseline · exact dynamic programming

10⁴³ schedules → ~10⁴ states, solved exactly

Storage depends only on the integer cumulative sum C_t = Σk_j, so exponentially many schedules fuse into an O(T²·L²) state lattice: exact and lossless.

Medium: 1.49×10¹⁸ candidates → 6,505 states, 0.02 s.
Large L7: 8.8×10⁴³ → 49,739 states, 0.20 s.
Verified: brute-force == DP on small (531,441).
Exact DP
Sub-second isn't a bug: medium DP does ~30k operations, not 10¹⁸. Optimal substructure collapses the exponential search to a polynomial lattice, state = (t, C_t, k_prev). This is our ground truth: every QUBO/QAOA is validated against it.
Result

What the optimizer actually does

Conserve (u = −2Δu) in ~11 of 26 weeks, else hold historical: redistributing releases in time, not just withholding water.

Lifts storage while spending 99% of the ±31 Mm³ balance budget.
Min storage 9.65% → 10.59% of capacity.
Feasible, ΔSRS = +0.021 vs. historical.
Optimal policy
Operational meaning: the balance constraint (spec Eq. 8) forbids simply releasing less all year: it forces redistribution. So the policy conserves during the deepest-deficit weeks and returns to historical otherwise. Modest gains, but honest given the drought: little slack to work with.

Benchmarking · robustness

Not an artifact of one time window

Re-run over 3 windows (first · middle · lowest-storage stress): medium beats historical in all three; debug/small stay u=0: the result is consistent, not a fluke of week 0.

Window robustness
Windows: first = weeks [0,T); middle = start (52−T)//2; stress = T-week window of lowest mean storage (deepest deficit). Plotting DP vs. historical per window separates "found the optimum" from "optimum beats the baseline." ΔSRS = 0 across debug/small (drought), > 0 across every medium window.
Quantum track

QUBO / QAOA: encoding the schedule

Every full instance exceeds ~30 qubits, so compact encodings + temporal chunking are necessary. Domain-wall is the encoding we ran on IBM hardware.

One-hot T·L; C_dev/C_smooth exact, balance 1-slack.
Binary halves qubits (T12/L3: 36→24, fits statevector).
Domain-wall 4 bits/week → IBM Quantum, 7+7+7+5 blocks.
Quantum · formulation
Encoding is a rubric point: same QUBO machinery, three interchangeable encodings, chosen by the qubit budget. Domain-wall (4 bits/week for L=5) ran on IBM Quantum in 7+7+7+5 blocks (≤30 q). Binary (24 q for T12/L3) fits a statevector simulator. Storage bounds dropped, balance exact 1-slack, C_crit soft.
Quantum track · results

Exact on real hardware at small scale, honest at scale

Debug T5/L3 reaches the exact optimum (AR = 1.000) on the simulator and real IBM hardware. The XY-mixer keeps 100% of probability one-hot-valid, making chunked blocks feasible.

Medium T26/L5: solved by chunking, 25 qubits/block.
Gap to full-DP = chunking + QAOA depth (separated).
Honest: no quantum advantage yet, expected (spec §7).
Quantum · results
Two real pipelines: our statevector one-hot/binary + XY-mixer, and ivan's domain-wall on IBM Quantum. Debug reaches the exact optimum on hardware (AR=1.0). The earlier chunked-medium was infeasible with a soft one-hot penalty; the XY-mixer fixes it by construction (every sample is valid one-hot), so blocks respect their balance budget. Goal isn't to beat DP: a correct, benchmarked, scalable quantum encoding. [Insert medium XY ΔSRS when the run lands.]
Conclusions

Best feasible ΔSRS = +0.021: a 6.8% cut in penalized cost

Formulation

26 weeks × 5 levels; SRS with 3 penalties and 4 constraints: every relaxation measured, not assumed.

Classical optimum

Exact DP, verified by brute force; conserve 11 weeks then hold. Runtime 0.02 s.

Scalability

10¹⁸ → 10⁴³ candidate schedules solved in < 1.1 s via cumulative-state reduction.

Quantum & limits

QAOA exact on hardware at debug; chunked + XY-mixer at medium. No advantage yet; simplified model (no treaty/legal terms).

Conclusions
Pros/cons (Presentation rubric): Pro: exact classical ground truth, honest quantum benchmarking, validated data, constraints justified by measurement. Con: modest ΔSRS (drought caps gains), p=1 QAOA can't beat DP, model omits legal/treaty complexity. Next: global-aware chunk balance, deeper QAOA (p>1).
